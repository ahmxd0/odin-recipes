Lamb and Rice

Description

This Lamb Over Rice recipe features tender, spiced lamb served over a bed of fragrant basmati rice. The lamb is marinated with a blend of spices, then slow-cooked until it’s perfectly tender and juicy. The rice is seasoned to complement the rich flavor of the lamb, making for a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Often served with a side of tangy yogurt sauce or a fresh cucumber salad, this dish is both comforting and filling. The combination of aromatic spices and hearty lamb makes it a popular choice in many Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines. Perfect for dinner, this recipe can also be served at gatherings or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 500g lamb shoulder, cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water or broth (chicken or vegetable)
  • 1 tbsp butter (optional, for the rice)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Yogurt sauce (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Season the lamb: In a bowl, season the lamb cubes with salt, pepper, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, and paprika. Toss to coat evenly.
  2. Brown the lamb: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned lamb cubes in batches and sear them until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes per batch. Remove the lamb and set it aside.
  3. Cook the onions and garlic: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and cook over medium heat until softened and golden, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Simmer the lamb: Return the lamb to the skillet with the onions and garlic. Add 1 cup of water or broth, and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the lamb is tender. Add more water or broth if needed to keep the lamb from drying out.
  5. Prepare the rice: While the lamb is cooking, rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water or broth to a boil. Add the rice and a pinch of salt. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and all the liquid is absorbed. If you’re using butter, stir it into the rice after it’s cooked.
  6. Assemble the dish: Once the lamb is tender, taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Fluff the rice with a fork, then serve a portion of rice on each plate. Top with the lamb and its cooking juices.
  7. Garnish and serve: Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro or parsley, and serve with optional yogurt sauce on the side for added flavor.
