Greek Salad

Description

This classic Greek Salad is a vibrant mix of fresh vegetables, tangy feta cheese, and briny Kalamata olives, all drizzled with a zesty olive oil and lemon dressing. It's the perfect balance of crunchy, creamy, and savory flavors, with a touch of Mediterranean flair. Whether you're serving it as a side dish, a light lunch, or alongside grilled meats, this salad is always a crowd-pleaser.

Full of healthy, colorful ingredients, the Greek Salad is naturally gluten-free and can be made vegan by omitting the feta cheese. With its simple ingredients and refreshing taste, it captures the essence of Greek cuisine and is a great way to add a burst of freshness to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cucumber, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 bell pepper (preferably green), sliced into thin strips
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 200g (7oz) block of feta cheese, cut into thick slices or crumbled
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Wash and dry the cucumber, tomatoes, and bell pepper. Cut the cucumber into thick slices, halve the tomatoes, and slice the bell pepper into thin rings or strips.
  2. Prepare the red onion: Peel the red onion and slice it thinly. If you prefer a milder onion flavor, you can soak the onion slices in cold water for 10-15 minutes to reduce the sharpness.
  3. Prepare the olives and cheese: Drain the Kalamata olives and set aside. Cut the block of feta cheese into thick slices or crumble it lightly into large chunks, depending on your preference.
  4. Assemble the salad: In a large bowl, combine the cucumber, tomatoes, bell pepper, and red onion. Add the Kalamata olives and top with the feta cheese.
  5. Prepare the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper. Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Toss the salad: Drizzle the dressing evenly over the salad and gently toss the ingredients together. Be careful not to break the feta too much.
  7. Serve: Garnish with a few extra olives, a sprinkle of dried oregano, and a drizzle of extra olive oil. Serve the salad chilled, alongside pita bread or as a side to grilled meats or seafood.
